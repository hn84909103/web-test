After Hour : Semiotics of the Bathroom

Videography . Video Editing . Animation . Logo Design

Overview

Semiotics of the Bathroom is a video project that using an uniqle way to introduce the Bopomofo transliteration system (commonly used in Taiwan). The video is inspired by Martha Rosler “Semiotics of the Kitchen” (1975) and produced by AFTER HOUR.

My Role

In the AFTER HOUR team, I was the Art Director, Videographer and Video Editor. The AFTER HOUR team is formed by three Taiwanese, Yu-Chen Lin, Amber Yuan and myself, the objective of the team is to produce something fun and free spirit in our after-work hours.

Story Behind the Scene

Three Taiwanese visited SUNY New Paltz Samuel Dorsky Museum of Art on a road trip during the 2017 Labor Day weekend, and we saw Martha Rosler's “Semiotics of the Kitchen” (1975).

In the video, Martha Rosler introduced the A to Z alphabet with kitchen appliences. Corresponding the Western alphabets, the common symbol we use in Taiwan is called Bopomofo which has 37 symbols. So, instead of kitchen applience, we use the items in the bathroom to introduce each Bopomofo symbol.
